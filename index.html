Anthony Vance, PhD

Bio

Anthony Vance is a professor in the Department of Business Information Technology at Virginia Tech. He earned Ph.D. degrees in Information Systems from Georgia State University, USA; the University of Parisâ€”Dauphine, France; and the University of Oulu, Finland. Previous to his PhD studies, he worked as a cybersecurity consultant at Deloitte.

His research focuses on how to help individuals and organizations to improve their cybersecurity posture, particularly from behavioral, organizational, and neuroscience perspectives. His work is published in outlets such as MIS Quarterly, Information Systems Research, Journal of Management Information Systems, Journal of the Association for Information Systems, Proceedings of the ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI), Workshop on the Economics of Information Security (WEIS), and the Symposium on Usable Privacy and Security (SOUPS). He currently is a senior editor at MIS Quarterly.

Tony is a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, and he and his wife Sarah are the parents of five children. Besides research and teaching, he enjoys the outdoors and spending time with his family. You can follow him on Twitter at @anthonyvance.
